Remember: When recording an impairment the impairment expense account increases and the property, plant and equipment account decreases.

Question: Barrick Gold Corp., the world’s largest gold miner by production, took $8.7 billion of write-downs in the second quarter of 2016 and slashed its dividend, in a stark example of the fallout from gold’s precipitous price decline. With gold prices down by more than 20%, Barrick joined a host of other miners forced to write down the value of mines acquired. The write-down (write-down is synonym with impairment loss) of $8.7 billion leads to (ignore depreciation expense effects):

  1. A decrease in the asset turnover (Sales / Average assets)

  2. A decrease in RoA (Net profit / Average assets)

  3. An increase in net cash from investing activities

  4. A decrease in net cash from investing activities

Solution: Point 2.
