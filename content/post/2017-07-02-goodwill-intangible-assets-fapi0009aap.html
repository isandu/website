Remember:

Goodwill = Purchase price - Fair market value of net assets purchased

Net assets = Assets - Liability or Net assets = Equity

Marriott International has completed in 2016 its $16,153 million purchase of Starwood. With the addition of Starwood’s portfolio the new Marriott has 1.1 million rooms in more than 110 countries, making Marriott the largest hotel company in the world. Before the merger Marriott reported intangible assets of $1,466 million. Starwood’s net assets fair value was an estimated $7,216 million before the merger, out of which $6,173 million were intangible assets.

Question 1

What is the value of goodwill recorded by Marriott as a result of the merger with Starwood? (amounts in $millions)

  1. $8,937

  2. $7,216

  3. $10,403

  4. $16,576

Solution:

Goodwill = Purchase price - fair market value of net assets

$8,937

Question 2

What is the total amount for intangibles recorded by Marriott at the end of 2016? (amounts in $millions)

  1. $8,937

  2. $7,216

  3. $10,403

  4. $16,576

Solution:

Total intangibles = Intangibles Marriot + intangibles Starwood + Goodwill

$16,576
