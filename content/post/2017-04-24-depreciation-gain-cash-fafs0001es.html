Remember: Cash from the sale of property, plant and equipment is equal to the value of the asset (net of amortization or depreciation) plus/minus the gain/loss.

In the indirect method the expenses (e.g., depreciation expense) or gains that do not involve cash are added to the net profit to find the cash from operating activities.

Cash flow from investing activities relates to the purchase and sale of property, plant and equipment and intangible assets.

During 2015 Castro Company buys patents for $10,972 (paid in cash) and a building for $95,935 (paid in cash). During 2015 the amortization expense of patents was $699 and the depreciation expense of the building was $4,446. Also during 2015 equipment with an initial cost of $54,464 and an accumulated depreciation of $20,084 was sold for a gain of $9,715 (cash transaction). Assume a net profit of zero.

Question: What would be the net cash flow from operating activities (through the indirect method)?

Solution: $-4,570

Question: What would be the net cash flow from investing activities?

Solution: $-151,002
