Remember: Cash (proceeds) from sale of property, plant and equipment = Value of the asset (net of amortization or depreciation) +/- Gain/Loss on sale.

In the indirect method of building the cash flow statement, the non-cash expenses (e.g., depreciation expense) or gains are added to the net profit to find the cash from operating activities.

Cash flow from investing activities relates to the purchase and sale of property, plant and equipment and intangible assets.

During 2015 Castro Company buys patents for $10,730 (paid in cash) and a building for $90,188 (paid in cash). During 2015 the amortization expense of patents was $668 and the depreciation expense of the building was $4,564. Also during 2015 equipment with an initial cost of $55,110 and an accumulated depreciation of $26,509 was sold for a gain of $9,518 (cash transaction). Assume a net profit of zero.

Question: What would be the net cash flow from operating activities (through the indirect method)?

Solution: $-4,286

Question: What would be the net cash flow from investing activities?

Solution: $-62,799
